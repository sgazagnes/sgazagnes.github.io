Executive Summary

ASML holds an effective monopoly on EUV lithography — the single technology enabling sub-5nm chip production. With a €36B backlog and 55%+ gross margins, the business is structurally exceptional. However, current valuation at 32x forward earnings leaves a compressed margin of safety. Recent order intake misses highlight vulnerability to cyclical foundry delays (specifically Intel), while geopolitical friction acts as a persistent headwind. For a medium-risk portfolio seeking quality compounders, ASML is a definitive core holding, but initiation should be staged. A disciplined entry in the €680–€720 zone provides a superior risk/reward ratio aligned with the 2024 consolidation base.

[RISK EVENT] A full escalation of U.S. export controls to China — historically representing ~20-29% of system sales — combined with ongoing Western foundry delays could compress near-term top-line growth and re-rate the multiple simultaneously.
